## 4 Eating

### 1 snack on something 

**mean:** eat small amounts of something between meals.

**Eg:** They're snacking on some crisps on the plane.

### 2 munch on something

**Mean:** eat snacks in casual (and noisy) ways

**Eg:** She is munching on an apple.

### 3 dig in / tuch in

Mean: start eating enthusiastically.

Eg: Everyone's got their plates? OK ——dig in!

Eg: The food smelled delicious - they couldn't wait to tuck in.

### 4 eat up / eat (something) up

Mean: finish everything on your plate.

Eg: Eat up! You've got a big day tomorrow - you'll need the energy.

Eg: You'd better eat up all your vegetables before you leave the table.

### 5 eat out

Mean: eat outside of your home. Such as a cafe or restaurant.

Eg: They had no food in the fridge, so they decided to eat out.
